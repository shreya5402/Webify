Spirited Away (2001)

Rating: 8.6 out of 10

Release year: 2001

Reviews: Spirited Away (Japanese: Sen to Chihiro no Kamikakushi) is a 2001 Japanese animated fantasy film written and directed by Hayao Miyazaki, animated by Studio Ghibli for Tokuma Shoten, Nippon Television Network. The film tells the story of a young girl, Chihiro, who gets trapped in a spirit world and must work in a bathhouse to free herself and her parents and return to the human world. The main voice cast of the movie include Rumi Hiiragi, Miyu Irino, Mari Natsuki, The film was a commercial and critical success and grossed over $289 million worldwide. It received widespread acclaim for its animation, storytelling, and emotional depth.The film won the Academy Award for Best Animated Feature and the Golden Bear at the 2002 Berlin International Film Festival, and it is considered one of the greatest anime films of all time. It's considered a masterpiece of Hayao Miyazaki and Studio Ghibli, known for their ability to make films that appeal to both adults and children with their stories that are both fantastical and emotionally resonant.
